# Request for Proposal: Enterprise Network Managed Services

**Issuing Authority:** Meridian County Government, Department of Information Technology
**RFP Reference:** RFP-MCG-2026-0714
**Issue Date:** July 1, 2026
**Submission Deadline:** August 15, 2026, 5:00 PM local time

## Background and Purpose

Meridian County Government ("the Authority") is seeking a qualified vendor to design, implement, and manage an enterprise-grade network infrastructure and support services contract covering all County administrative sites. This Request for Proposal (RFP) sets out the Authority's mandatory requirements below. Bidders must respond to each clause in Section 1 individually and provide verifiable evidence of compliance; general statements of capability without supporting evidence may be scored as partially compliant or non-compliant at the Authority's discretion.

## 1. Mandatory Service, Support, and Compliance Requirements

### Clause 1.1 — Uptime Guarantee (Ref: C1)

The Bidder's proposed solution must guarantee a minimum of 99.9% network uptime, measured on a calendar-month basis and calculated exclusive of pre-approved maintenance windows not exceeding four (4) hours per month. The Bidder must submit documented evidence of the redundancy architecture supporting this commitment, including but not limited to multi-region failover design, and must specify the service credit remedy applicable should this threshold not be met in any given month.

### Clause 1.2 — Customer Support Standards (Ref: C2)

The Bidder shall provide outstanding customer support and demonstrate a strong commitment to fast response times across all support tickets raised by the Authority during the contract term, ensuring a consistently high standard of client satisfaction.

### Clause 1.3 — Information Security Compliance (Ref: C3)

The Bidder's information security management system, including all systems and processes used to store, process, or transmit the Authority's data, must be certified to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (or any successor version). Bidders must submit a copy of their current, valid ISO 27001 certificate as part of their technical proposal; proposals lacking a valid certificate at the time of submission will be deemed non-compliant with this clause.

## 2. Submission Guidelines

Proposals must be submitted electronically in PDF format no later than the submission deadline stated above. Late submissions will not be considered. Each proposal must include a compliance matrix cross-referencing every clause in Section 1 with the corresponding page and section of the Bidder's technical response.

## 3. Evaluation Criteria

Proposals will be evaluated on technical compliance (50%), commercial competitiveness (30%), and vendor track record (20%). Technical compliance scoring will weight verifiable, evidence-backed responses more heavily than general statements of capability.
